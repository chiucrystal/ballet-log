## Current Top 3 Priorities
*Updated: 23 June 2026*

1. **Weight placement and demi-pointe proprioception** — toes leave floor on demi-pointe (bilateral, physio-confirmed), heels gripping to maintain turnout anchors weight backward, sitting back in heels even in fifth. Upstream of pirouette axis, winging, ankle instability, and retiré mechanics. Must be reliable on flat and in slow rises before pointe and jumps can return.
2. **Left lateral chain / bilateral ankle stability** — left lateral chain weakness confirmed at baseline (present on flat, not just under load). Left side carrying 15+ weeks of compensatory load from right ankle recovery. Drives torso collapse, winging, hip hiking, retiré instability, pirouette axis failure. Right ankle plié range still restricted.
3. **Barre technique and retiré mechanics** — frappés, fondu, grand battement, rond de jambe all showing active corrections across June sessions. Retiré being built foot-up rather than hip-down: glute not activating, knee not lifting, hip rotating forward. Current restrictions are the window to fix this before jumps and pointe return.

## Syllabus Corrections

### Barre work
- Grand plié in first: don't go so low; shoulders don't come forward; core engaged, don't collapse. Affects **correct posture**
- Tendus: toes stay completely on the floor when returning to fifth. Affects **line**
- Weight even in plié in fourth for tendus and pliés. Affects **correct posture and weight placement**
- Fondu: thigh leads rather than heel or foot; coupé low and on top of ankle; find resistance at the fondu to demi-pointe. Affects **control** and **dynamic values**
- Fondu: lengthen supporting side especially in second and derrière; leg directly behind hip in derrière; sequence is fondu + attitude before demi-pointe. Affects **co-ordination**
- Rond de jambe jeté: aligned in front and behind; timing too quick. Affects **co-ordination** and **dynamic values**
- Rond en l'air: accent and resistance more emphasised; sharper coming in. Affects **dynamic values**
- Frappés: losing accent and dynamic on the en dedans; accent and hold not sustained en croix. Affects **dynamic values**
- Coupé under and over (frappés and glissades): both feet must point fully. Affects **line**
- Grand battement: weight sitting back in devant; don't curl toes. Affects **correct posture and weight placement**
- Grand battement: passé sickled — glute not activating, knee not lifting; toes scoop and articulate before passé. Affects **co-ordination** and **line**
- Grand battement: don't hike working hip to the side; don't twist shoulders. Affects **line**
- General barre: hands lightly on barre — no weight on it; supporting side engaged, weight over toes, arches pulling up. Affects **correct posture**
- Move upper body over the toes in derrière; don't contract the neck. Affects **line** and **expression**
- Use all the music at the barre — don't finish phrases early. Affects **dynamic values**

### Port de bras
- Weight forward on the pivot. Affects **correct posture and weight placement**
- Arms to first then open to fourth faster to get around. Affects **co-ordination** and **dynamic values**
- Bottom leg turns out fully. Affects **co-ordination**
- Glissade: point second foot especially devant; barely elevate but have a sense of lift. Affects **line** and **dynamic values**
- Step-unfold: currently unstable — cause not yet identified. Flag for investigation.
- Eyeline: wider fourth position; follows the hands; more peripheral space. Affects **expression**
- Back heel down in the back bend. Affects **line**

### AF-10: Centre practice and pirouettes
- Pirouettes closing in 3rd not 5th — axis collapses before rotation completes. Affects **control** and **dynamic values**
- Arm-driven rotation rather than hip-initiated; shoulder wind-up especially in en dedans. Affects **co-ordination**
- Pirouette preparation: weight not fully forward in fondu — dorsiflexion restriction is load-dependent, not structural. Affects **correct posture and weight placement**
- Passé dropping at 1.5 turns mid-rotation — anchor failing on transition to second turn. Affects **control**
- Heels landing at same time rather than dropping into closed fifth. Affects **control**
- Think going up in the turn; lock standing knee and pull up. Affects **control**
- *Progress: good double achieved May 2026; solid singles confirmed June 2026*

- Lame ducks: not tight, weight back. Affects **correct posture and weight placement**
- En dedans: back leg not coming in fast enough — passé retiré delay. Affects **dynamic values**

## Training Notes
- **Weight forward baseline:** 2/3 weight over ball of foot, 1/3 heel — established reference for "over your feet"
- **Toes leaving floor check:** if toes can lift on demi-pointe, weight is behind the axis. Use as a real-time proprioceptive check.
- **Turnout from femur not heel:** forcing turnout by gripping with heels anchors weight backward. Find turnout from the femur rotating in the socket — heels should be free.
- **Retiré — hip down, not foot up:** glute activates to hold the knee, knee lifts, toes scoop last. Fixing the hip first resolves sickling downstream.
- **Left lateral chain on flat:** weakness present in neutral standing. "Left side long before anything else moves" as a pre-movement cue. Left hand on left waist to monitor the space.
- **Thoracic stiffness:** thoracic extension restricted — lumbar compensates in arabesques and backbends. Upper back extension Pilates work is targeting this. Don't stretch into arabesque range without confirming thoracic, not lumbar, is working.
- **Pelvic alignment improving** as of May 2026 — maintain. Proprioceptive sense of neutral still unreliable — use hands on hip points and sacrum. Mirror not reliable due to fuller glutes causing misreads.
- **Anticipation in second rotation:** expecting not to get around kills the second turn. "Intend for three, get two" — remove the ceiling.
- **Progress markers:** good double pirouette May 2026; solid singles June 2026; heels arriving together in coordination fixed June 2026.
